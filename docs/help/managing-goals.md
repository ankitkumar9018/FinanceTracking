# Managing Investment Goals

This guide explains how to set financial goals in FinanceTracker and track your progress toward them.

---

## What Are Investment Goals?

A goal is a financial target you want to reach by a certain date. For example:
- Save 25 lakh for a house down payment by December 2027
- Build a 1 crore retirement corpus by 2040
- Save 5 lakh for your child's education by 2030
- Save 2,000 EUR for a vacation by next summer

FinanceTracker helps you set these goals, link them to your investments, and visually track how close you are to achieving them.

---

## Creating a Goal

1. Go to **Goals** from the sidebar
2. Click **+ New Goal**
3. Fill in the details:

| Field | What to Enter | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Goal Name** | A descriptive name | "House Down Payment" |
| **Target Amount** | How much you need | 25,00,000 |
| **Target Date** | When you need it by | December 31, 2027 |
| **Category** | Choose from the list | House |
| **Linked Portfolio** | (Optional) Which portfolio to track against | "Indian Stocks" |

4. Click **Create**

### Goal Categories

You can choose from:
- **Retirement** -- Long-term retirement savings
- **House** -- Buying a house or property
- **Education** -- Children's education or personal upskilling
- **Travel** -- Vacation fund
- **Emergency** -- Emergency fund (typically 6-12 months of expenses)
- **Custom** -- Anything else (car, wedding, business, etc.)

Each category has a different icon to help you identify your goals at a glance.

## Understanding the Goal Dashboard

Each goal shows:

### Progress Gauge

A visual circular gauge that fills up as you get closer to your target. It shows:
- Your current amount (how much you have invested toward this goal)
- Your target amount
- Percentage complete

### Monthly SIP Needed

The app calculates how much you need to invest every month (via SIP -- Systematic Investment Plan) to reach your goal on time, assuming a reasonable rate of return.

For example:
> "To reach 25,00,000 by Dec 2027, you need to invest approximately 55,000 per month"

This number updates as your investments grow (or shrink).

### Time Remaining

Shows how much time is left until your target date, in months and days.

### Status Indicators

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **On Track** (green) | You are investing enough to reach your goal on time |
| **Needs Attention** (yellow) | You are slightly behind and may need to increase your SIP |
| **Behind** (red) | You are significantly behind schedule |
| **Achieved** (celebration!) | Congratulations! You have reached your target |

## Linking a Portfolio to a Goal

When you link a portfolio to a goal, the app automatically tracks the portfolio's current value as your progress.

1. When creating or editing a goal, select a portfolio from the **Linked Portfolio** dropdown
2. The goal's "current amount" will automatically update to match the portfolio's total value
3. As your investments grow, the progress gauge updates automatically

### Do I Have to Link a Portfolio?

No. You can also update the "current amount" manually. This is useful if:
- Your savings for the goal are spread across multiple portfolios
- Some of the money is in a bank account, not in stocks
- You want to track a portion of a portfolio, not the entire thing

## Milestone Celebrations

FinanceTracker celebrates your progress with fun animations at key milestones:

| Milestone | What Happens |
|---|---|
| **25% reached** | A small celebration animation appears |
| **50% reached** | Halfway there! A larger celebration animation |
| **75% reached** | Almost there! Animation with congratulations |
| **100% reached** | Full confetti celebration! Goal marked as achieved |

These celebrations are designed to keep you motivated on your financial journey.

---

## Editing a Goal

1. Go to **Goals**
2. Click on the goal you want to change
3. Click **Edit**
4. Update any field (name, amount, date, category, portfolio)
5. Click **Save**

You can adjust your target amount or date as your circumstances change. The monthly SIP needed will recalculate automatically.

---

## Deleting a Goal

1. Go to **Goals**
2. Click on the goal
3. Click **Delete**
4. Confirm the deletion

Deleting a goal does not affect your portfolio or investments -- it only removes the goal tracker.

---

## Tips for Goal-Based Investing

1. **Be realistic**: Set target amounts and dates that are achievable based on your income and savings rate
2. **Start early**: The earlier you start, the less you need to save each month (thanks to compounding)
3. **Review regularly**: Check your goals monthly and adjust if needed
4. **Separate portfolios**: Consider creating a separate portfolio for each major goal (e.g., one for retirement, one for house)
5. **Emergency fund first**: Make sure you have 6-12 months of expenses in a liquid fund before focusing on other goals
