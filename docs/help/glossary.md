# Financial Glossary

A simple dictionary of financial and investment terms used in FinanceTracker. Definitions are written in plain language for non-technical users.

---

## A

### Abgeltungssteuer
The German flat tax on investment income (capital gains, dividends, interest). The rate is 25% plus a 5.5% solidarity surcharge, totaling 26.375%. See [Tax Features](tax-features.md).

### Average Price
The weighted average of all your purchase prices for a stock. If you bought 50 shares at 100 and 50 shares at 120, your average price is 110. FinanceTracker calculates this automatically.

---

## B

### Base Level
The lowest price level you set for a stock in FinanceTracker. If the price drops to or below this level, the stock shows a dark red alert. Think of it as your "floor" or "strong support" line.

### Beta
A measure of how much a stock moves compared to the overall market. A beta of 1 means it moves with the market. Above 1 means it is more volatile. Below 1 means it is less volatile.

### Bollinger Bands
Three lines on a chart: a middle line (20-day average) and an upper and lower band (2 standard deviations away). The price usually stays inside the bands. When it touches the edges, it might be temporarily overbought or oversold.

### BSE
Bombay Stock Exchange. One of the two main stock exchanges in India. Symbols for BSE stocks end in .BO when used in data feeds (e.g., RELIANCE.BO).

---

## C

### Candlestick
A type of chart bar that shows four prices: open, high, low, and close. Green candles mean the price went up; red candles mean the price went down. See [Reading Charts](reading-charts.md).

### CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate)
The average yearly growth rate of your investment, accounting for compounding. If you invested 100,000 and it became 150,000 in 3 years, the CAGR is about 14.5%.

### CAS (Consolidated Account Statement)
A document from CAMS or KFintech that lists all your mutual fund holdings across all fund houses. FinanceTracker can import this to track your mutual funds.

### Cumulative Quantity
The total number of shares you currently hold in a stock. Calculated as: total bought minus total sold.

---

## D

### Dark Green
A color in the FinanceTracker alert system. It means the stock price has reached or exceeded your top level (target price). See [Understanding Alerts](understanding-alerts.md).

### Dark Red
A color in the FinanceTracker alert system. It means the stock price has dropped to or below your base level (critical support). See [Understanding Alerts](understanding-alerts.md).

### Dividend
A payment made by a company to its shareholders, usually from profits. For example, if a company declares a dividend of 10 per share and you hold 100 shares, you receive 1,000.

### DRIP (Dividend Reinvestment Plan)
When your dividend is automatically used to buy more shares of the same stock instead of being paid as cash.

---

## E

### EMA (Exponential Moving Average)
Similar to SMA, but gives more weight to recent prices. The EMA-12 reacts faster to price changes than the SMA-12. Used in technical analysis to identify trends.

### Efficient Frontier
A concept from Modern Portfolio Theory. It is a curve that shows the best possible return for every level of risk. FinanceTracker can calculate this to suggest how to rebalance your portfolio.

### Exchange
The marketplace where stocks are traded. Common exchanges: NSE (National Stock Exchange, India), BSE (Bombay Stock Exchange, India), XETRA (Germany's electronic exchange).

---

## F

### Fibonacci Retracement
Horizontal lines on a chart based on mathematical ratios (23.6%, 38.2%, 50%, 61.8%). Traders use these levels to predict where a stock might find support or resistance.

### Freistellungsauftrag
A German tax exemption order. It tells your broker to not deduct tax on the first 1,000 EUR (or 2,000 EUR for couples) of investment income per year.

### FIFO (First In, First Out)
A method for determining which shares you "sold" when calculating gains. The shares you bought first are considered sold first. Used for tax calculations.

---

## G-H

### Grandfathering (Indian LTCG)
For shares bought before February 1, 2018, the purchase price used for LTCG tax calculation is the higher of the actual purchase price or the fair market value as of January 31, 2018. This rule "grandfathers" old purchases to avoid retroactive taxation.

### HSTS
HTTP Strict Transport Security. A security feature that forces browsers to use HTTPS. Protects your data in transit.

---

## I-J

### ISIN
International Securities Identification Number. A unique 12-character code that identifies a stock globally. Example: INE002A01018 (Reliance Industries).

### ITR (Income Tax Return)
The annual tax filing required in India. FinanceTracker generates data that helps you or your CA fill out the ITR.

### JWT (JSON Web Token)
The authentication method used by FinanceTracker. When you log in, you receive a token that proves your identity for subsequent requests. Technical detail -- you do not need to know this to use the app.

---

## K-L

### Kirchensteuer
German church tax. If you are a registered member of a church in Germany, an additional 8% or 9% of your capital gains tax is charged as church tax.

### Light Green
A color in the FinanceTracker alert system. It means the stock price has entered the upper mid range (opportunity zone). See [Understanding Alerts](understanding-alerts.md).

### Light Red
A color in the FinanceTracker alert system. It means the stock price has entered the lower mid range (caution zone). See [Understanding Alerts](understanding-alerts.md).

### LTCG (Long-Term Capital Gains)
Profits from selling stocks held for 12 months or more (India). Taxed at 12.5% on gains above 1,25,000 per year. See [Tax Features](tax-features.md).

---

## M

### MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)
A technical indicator that shows momentum and trend direction. It consists of two lines (MACD and Signal) and a histogram. When the MACD line crosses above the Signal line, it may indicate a buying opportunity.

### Max Drawdown
The largest percentage drop from a peak to a trough in your portfolio value. If your portfolio went from 10,00,000 to 8,00,000 at its worst point, the max drawdown is 20%. Lower is better.

### Mid Range
In FinanceTracker, the zones between your normal price range and the extremes (base/top). There are four mid range values: lower mid range 1 and 2, upper mid range 1 and 2. See [Understanding Alerts](understanding-alerts.md).

---

## N

### NAV (Net Asset Value)
The per-unit value of a mutual fund. Similar to the "share price" of a stock. NAV is updated once per day for Indian mutual funds.

### NSE (National Stock Exchange)
The primary stock exchange in India. Symbols for NSE stocks end in .NS when used in data feeds (e.g., RELIANCE.NS, TCS.NS).

---

## O

### OHLCV
Stands for Open, High, Low, Close, Volume. These are the five key data points recorded for each trading day (or candle) of a stock.

### Overbought
When a stock's RSI is above 70, it is considered "overbought." This means the stock has risen a lot recently and might be due for a pullback. It is not a guarantee of a price drop.

### Oversold
When a stock's RSI is below 30, it is considered "oversold." This means the stock has fallen a lot recently and might bounce back. It is not a guarantee of a price increase.

---

## P

### P&L (Profit and Loss)
The difference between your current portfolio value and what you invested. Positive P&L = profit. Negative P&L = loss. Can be expressed as an amount or a percentage.

### PSD2 (Payment Services Directive 2)
A European Union regulation that requires banks to provide third-party access to account data through APIs. This is how FinanceTracker connects to German brokers like Deutsche Bank and comdirect.

---

## R

### RSI (Relative Strength Index)
A momentum indicator that ranges from 0 to 100. It measures how fast and how much a stock's price has been changing. Below 30 = potentially oversold. Above 70 = potentially overbought. FinanceTracker uses RSI-14 (14-day period).

---

## S

### Sharpe Ratio
A measure of risk-adjusted return. It tells you how much return you earn for each unit of risk. A Sharpe ratio above 1 is considered good, above 2 is very good, and below 0 means you are losing money.

Formula: (Portfolio Return - Risk-Free Rate) / Portfolio Volatility

### SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)
A method of investing a fixed amount regularly (usually monthly) into a mutual fund or stock. SIPs help you benefit from rupee-cost averaging.

### SMA (Simple Moving Average)
The average closing price over a number of days. SMA-20 = average of the last 20 days. SMA-50 = average of the last 50 days. Used on charts to show trends.

### Sortino Ratio
Similar to the Sharpe Ratio, but only considers downside risk (losses). A higher Sortino ratio means better risk-adjusted returns with less downside volatility.

### STCG (Short-Term Capital Gains)
Profits from selling stocks held for less than 12 months (India). Taxed at 20%. See [Tax Features](tax-features.md).

### STT (Securities Transaction Tax)
A tax charged on every stock transaction in India. It is typically included in your brokerage fees. STT paid on buy can be claimed against STCG when filing taxes.

---

## T

### TDS (Tax Deducted at Source)
Tax automatically deducted before you receive income. For dividends in India, 10% TDS is deducted if total dividends from one company exceed 5,000 per year.

### Top Level
The highest price level you set for a stock in FinanceTracker. If the price reaches or exceeds this level, the stock shows a dark green alert. Think of it as your "target price" or "ceiling."

### TOTP (Time-based One-Time Password)
A 6-digit code that changes every 30 seconds, generated by an app like Google Authenticator. Used for two-factor authentication (2FA) in FinanceTracker and some brokers.

---

## V

### VaR (Value at Risk)
A statistical measure that estimates the maximum potential loss of your portfolio over a given time period with a given confidence level. "95% VaR of 50,000" means there is a 95% chance your loss will not exceed 50,000 in the given period.

### Vorabpauschale
A German tax concept for accumulating funds. A notional income is calculated annually based on the fund's value and the ECB base rate. You pay tax on this notional income even though you did not actually receive any cash.

### Volatility
How much a stock's price fluctuates over time. High volatility means big price swings (up and down). Low volatility means the price is relatively stable. Usually measured as the standard deviation of returns.

---

## W-X

### Wash Sale
In some countries (like the USA), selling a stock at a loss and buying it back within 30 days means you cannot claim the loss for tax purposes. **India does not have a wash sale rule**, so you can sell and immediately rebuy for tax harvesting.

### XETRA
Germany's electronic stock exchange, operated by Deutsche Boerse. Most German stocks and ETFs are traded here. Symbols end in .DE (e.g., SAP.DE).

### XIRR (Extended Internal Rate of Return)
A way to calculate your actual investment return that accounts for the timing and amount of each cash flow (SIP, lump sum, withdrawal). More accurate than simple percentage returns when you have invested at different times.
