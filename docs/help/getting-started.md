# Getting Started

Welcome to FinanceTracker! This guide will walk you through setting up your account and getting your portfolio into the app for the first time.

---

## Step 1: Create Your Account

When you open the app for the first time, you will see the login page.

1. Click **Register** (or "Create Account")
2. Fill in your details:
   - **Email**: Your email address (used for login and email notifications)
   - **Password**: Choose a strong password (at least 8 characters)
   - **Display Name**: Your name as you want it shown in the app
   - **Preferred Currency**: Choose INR (Indian Rupees) or EUR (Euros)
3. Click **Create Account**
4. You will be logged in automatically

## Step 2: Choose Your Setup Method

After logging in for the first time, a setup wizard will guide you. You have three options for getting your stocks into the app:

### Option A: Import from Excel (Fastest if you already track in a spreadsheet)

If you have an existing Excel file with your stock data, this is the quickest way. Go to the **Import** section and upload your .xlsx file. The app will read your stocks, quantities, and prices automatically. See the [Importing Data](importing-data.md) help article for detailed instructions.

### Option B: Add Stocks Manually

Click the **+ Add Stock** button on the dashboard. Search for a stock by name or symbol, enter your purchase details, and save. Repeat for each stock. This is a good option if you have just a few stocks.

### Option C: Connect Your Broker (Automatic)

If you have an account with Zerodha, ICICI Direct, Angel One, or another supported broker, you can connect it and your holdings will sync automatically. Go to **Settings** then **Brokers** to set this up. See the [Connecting Brokers](connecting-brokers.md) help article for step-by-step instructions.

You can combine all three methods. For example, import your old stocks from Excel and then connect your broker for future automatic syncing.

## Step 3: Set Your Price Ranges

For each stock, you can set price ranges that tell the app when to alert you:

- **Base Level**: The lowest price you are comfortable with (strong support level)
- **Lower Mid Range 1 and 2**: A zone where the price is getting low (caution zone)
- **Upper Mid Range 1 and 2**: A zone where the price is getting high (opportunity to sell)
- **Top Level**: Your target price (where you might consider selling)

You can set these during import, when adding a stock, or by clicking directly on the values in the portfolio table to edit them.

If you are not sure what values to use, you can leave them blank for now and add them later.

## Step 4: Explore the Dashboard

Your dashboard now shows your portfolio with:
- **Summary cards** at the top showing your total value and today's gains/losses
- **Holdings table** with current prices, RSI values, and color-coded alerts
- **Charts** -- click any stock to see its price chart

## Step 5: Optional Configuration

When you are ready, you can also:
- **Set up notifications**: Get alerts via email, WhatsApp, Telegram, or SMS when stocks enter your price ranges
- **Enable the AI assistant**: Ask questions about your portfolio in plain English
- **Configure your display**: Choose dark or light theme, customize which columns appear in the table

All of these are available from the **Settings** page.
